OUR

FOUNDING

Phi Delta Theta's Pennsylvania Pi Chapter was founded April 27, 2001 on the campus of Robert Morris University by 20 college men. Pennsylvania Pi's chapter has tripled in size since its founding. Our founding fathers are listed below. Since gaining University status one year later after our founding, RMU has become home to 193 Phis over the past fourteen years. The RMU Phis have accomplished several goals in recent years, including winning the 2013 Kansas City Trophy and endowing a scholarship in memory of founding father Michael R. Haines.

OUR

CAMPUS

Robert Morris University is dedicated to changing the lives of its students so that they can go out and change the lives of others. A private, suburban university near Pittsburgh, RMU combines the advantages of a safe residential campus with the opportunities for experiential learning, professional internships, and networking opportunities of a large city. The university combines academic excellence with a professional focus in more than 60 undergraduate and 20 graduate degree programs. RMU is a pioneer in its emphasis on engaged learning. OUR

GREEK LIFE

The mission of Greek Life at Robert Morris University is to uphold our common values of Leadership, Service, Brotherhood, Sisterhood, and Scholarship every day by all members of fraternity and sorority chapters. The Office of Student Life pledges to work with all community stakeholders to cultivate an environment that is in congruence with and fosters a commitment to the core values of our respective organizations and Robert Morris University. It is our vision to create a model community of fraternities and sororities at Robert Morris University that achieves national recognition for its commitment to honoring shared values, building empowered students, and creating engaged and successful alumni. When fraternity and sorority is done right, we believe there is no better comprehensive student development experience in higher education.

OUR

PARENTS

Our parents support us in everything we do, this includes being a member of Phi Delta Theta. Our parents realize that being apart of Phi Delta Theta is not a pledge to a "frat" but to a fraternity of upstanding gentleman. We work everyday to become the greatest versions of ourselves and our parents notice that goal and encourage us on our passage. Parents are urged to reach out to the chapter if they have any concerns with a son joining in order to ensure support from all corners of a member during his four years at college.
