Brothers Endow Haines Scholarship at the 2015 Alumni Dinner

During this years Alumni Dinner on February 22nd, our chapter announced the completion of endowment for the Michael Haines Scholarship. Haines was a fraternity brother of Phi Delta Theta at Robert Morris University. Mike was a victim of an armored truck heist in 2012, and the chapter has raised over $25,000 in just over two years to fund a scholarship that would be awarded in brother Haines name. "It's been a great recognition of Mike, but it has also been a great display of character on the part of the fraternity. It says a great deal about the young men who have worked so hard," Ann Haines, Michael's mother, said. Recipients of the scholarship must demonstrate the principles of the fraternity, friendship, sound learning, and rectitude.

Brothers Attend Presidents Council Dinner in Downtown Pittsburgh

Close to 300 members of the President's Council of Robert Morris University attended the annual President's Council Dinner in December at Heinz Field, once again setting an attendance record for an event that grows year after year. Among, the attendees were many current members and Alumni of Phi Delta Theta. As the president gave his State of the University address, RMU's most valued supporters and friends got to enjoy dinner with the university's most promising seniors nominated for the prestigious Rising Star award.
